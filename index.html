What is your previous experience?


Growing up, I watched both my parents work in financial institutes where they devoted efforts to make sure that they succeeded at any given task. And so, due to their subliminal influence, I became inclined to engage in a related field.

Suffice to say that during my undergraduate studies, I always excelled brilliantly at mathematically related courses such as linear algebra and analytic geometry, probability theory, mathematical analysis for engineers and mathematical statistics. After partaking various mathematical contests in modeling, my interest was inspired to solve problems in real-life situations by making use of my knowledge and skills. With my curiosity and attraction to solving factual cases in the systematic thinking with up to date modeling tools, I studied in the Engineering and Policy Analysis Program at Delft University of Technology.

After graduation, my job at the Xiamen International Trust Co ltd was centered around the quantitative analysis of the companies and macro industry, and designed financial products accordingly.

Why do you want to be a designer?


It does not start at any specific point because design is always my passion. When I felt that I did not have that kind of passion fo what I was doing at work, I thought that something had to be changed. If I want to make continuous progress and realize my own value, I should go to a career that I love.

I don't think there's a big gap between my previous experience and becoming a designer. A trend appears that being a designer requires plenty of experiences in different fields. So my previous experience is my advantage. Besides, I really love learning and that is what a designer should always do.

Why do you expect of your future?


Now I am striving to become a better designer every day and my life goal is to use design to make a better world!
